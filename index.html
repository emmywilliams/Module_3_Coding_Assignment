Black Sigatoka is a fungal disease that affects banana plants and is considered a major threat to banana production worldwide. The disease spreads rapidly, causing significant yield losses and reducing the quality of the fruit. One potential solution to mitigate the spread of Black Sigatoka is through the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems that can detect the disease early on and enable effective management and control measures. In constrained resource settings, such as smallholder farms and rural communities, there may be limitations on the availability of resources, such as computing power, internet connectivity, and technical expertise. Therefore, any AI system designed for Black Sigatoka detection in these settings must be efficient, cost-effective, and easy to use.
